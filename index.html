Training

I spent this past summer mainly base-building, because I fucking hate HATE **HATE** hot weather, and it's all I can do to just slog through my 50 miles per week when the air is the temperature and consistency of freshly-made soup. Got in lots of hills, long trail runs, and reluctant heat training.

Once fall rolled around, I started focusing a but more on getting my speed back, while simulataneously building up miles to be ready to pace my little sister for her first marathon in Chicago back in October. Once my pacing duties had concluded and I had successfully ushered my sister across the finish line, it was time to fully focus on preparation for this race.

I used a shortened and modified version of the Higdon Advanced I training plan, jumping in around week 10 of the plan since I was already up to 50+ mpw. I topped out at about 65 miles for my peak week, with 5 20-milers in my training cycle and a couple of 10+ mile pace runs to get my body ready to move fast for hours on end. After one last 20-mile long run the day after Christmas, I began my taper.
